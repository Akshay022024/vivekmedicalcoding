STEP BY STEP PROCESS

1. Enroll in the Program

Requirements:

  • Education:
    Most programs require a high school diploma or equivalent. Some may recommend an associate degree.

  • Application:
    Complete the application process, it may include submitting transcripts and other documentation.

    • 2. Research and Choose a Training Program

    Types of Training Programs:

  • Online Courses:
    Flexible and accessible, suitable for working professionals or those with busy schedules.

  • In-Person Classes:
    Offered by community colleges, universities, and vocational schools, providing hands-on experience and direct interaction with instructors.

    • Factors to Consider:

  • Accreditation:

    Ensure that the program is accredited by recognized bodies like the AAPC or AHIMA (American Health Information Management Association).

  • Curriculum:

    Look for a comprehensive curriculum covering ICD-10-CM, CPT, HCPCS, medical terminology, anatomy, and healthcare reimbursement.

  • Cost:

    Compare tuition fees and additional costs, such as textbooks, exam fees, and membership fees.

  • Duration:

    Consider the length of the program and whether it fits your timeline.

  • Reputation:

    Check reviews, testimonials, and job placement rates of the program.

    • 3. Complete the Training

    Coursework:

  • Medical Terminology

    Understanding the language used in healthcare.

  • Anatomy and Physiology:

    Learning about the human body and its systems.

  • Coding Systems

    In-depth training in ICD-10-CM, CPT, and HCPCS coding systems.

  • Healthcare Reimbursement:

    Knowledge of billing processes, insurance claims, and compliance.

    • Practical Experience:

  • Coding Practice:

    Engage in practical coding exercises and real-world scenarios to apply coding principles.

  • Internships/Practicums:

    Some programs offer internships or practicums for hands-on experience in a healthcare setting.

    • 4. Prepare for Certification

    Certification Exam:

  • CPC (Certified Professional Coder):

    Offered by the AAPC, focusing on physician-based coding.

  • CCA (Certified Coding Associate) and CCS (Certified Coding Specialist):

    Offered by AHIMA, focusing on a broader range of coding environments.

    • Study Materials:

  • Study Guides and Textbooks:

    Official study guides and textbooks provided by AAPC or AHIMA.

  • Online Resources:

    Practice exams, quizzes, and coding forums.

  • Review Courses:

    Some training programs offer specific review courses for certification exams.

    • 5. Register for the Exam

    Exam Registration:

  • AAPC Membership:

    Required to take the CPC exam. Membership provides access to resources and networking opportunities.

  • Exam Fees:

    Pay the exam fee, which typically includes one free retake within a year if needed.

  • Schedule the Exam:

    Choose a convenient exam date and location.

    • 6. Take and Pass the Certification Exam

    Exam Preparation:

  • Practice Exams:

    Take multiple practice exams to familiarize yourself with the format and timing.

  • Review Sessions:

    Attend review sessions or study groups to reinforce your knowledge.

    • Exam Day:

  • Materials:

    Bring allowed materials, such as coding manuals and identification.

  • Time Management:

    Organize your time effectively during the exam to answer all questions.

    • 7. Continue Professional Development

    Continuing Education:

  • CEUs (Continuing Education Units):

    Earn CEUs to maintain your certification and stay updated with coding changes and industry trends.

  • AAPC or AHIMA Membership:

    Maintain membership for ongoing education, networking, and professional growth.

    • Career Opportunities:

  • Job Search:

    Utilize job placement services offered by your training program or professional organizations.

  • Networking:

    Attend industry conferences, workshops, and local chapter meetings to network with other professionals and potential employers.
